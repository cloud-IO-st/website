Privacy Policy

Whatever information you reveal, you do it at your own will. This helps us remove all unnecessary processes and regulations giving us more room for collaboration and communication. Licences
Copy Rights, Trademarks and Ownership

First off we only accept and work with projects that use BSD derived licenses which are compatible with the Apache 2 License. There's no games with dual licensing schemes here to run a business. Do that somewhere else and we sincerely wish you luck. If you want to commercialize the products here do it somewhere else with your own trademarks. But SSF is not a place for marketing gimmicks designed to abuse OS.


We do not allow projects to dual license or use any viral licenses designed to limit downstream users. Stick with the AL 2.0 and you'll be fine.


With that said you own the copy rights, but SSF owns the marks so you and everyone else can have it free forever under these licenses. Commercial companies can come and use the code, or fork it, and re-brand it, but they need to use their own marks. The only thing we ask is that commercial versions make mention of SSF product being used in their product whatever their product's name is. This is to prevent people from turning SSF into a commercial tool. The software here is for anyone. And bravo to you if you do make money from us, then we hope you'll contribute back to help support the community that put some coin in your pocket.


We do tolerate companies that pop up to support SSF Software commercially. They just need to be careful not to make it seem as though the SSF mark is theirs. If having your own product at your own company is so important just fork the code and re-brand. Otherwise be clear that the SSF product you're selling support for is not your company's product. You can however say that y'all created the project at SSF and that your company's engineers are developers there. That's totally fine too.


To keep the code base clean and free of IP so we can use the AL 2.0 we have to have each contributor at SSF at least sign a CLA.
