Our Mission

The Subutai Social Foundation (SSF) is an open technology and innovation foundation with the primary aim of building social, collaborative, and meritocratic communities around commercially friendly open source software and open hardware projects. Based on an exciting new platform and approach to social innovation the SSF improves the way we collaborate and share knowledge to maximize our collective innovative capacity. Discover what we have to offer: come join an existing project or create a new project hosted at the SSF.

Analytics for Meritocracy

Many foundations talk about operating as a meritocracy but often, without the proper metrics, this governance model degrades to ugly majority rule dynamics. Talkers with their fans emerge as bureaucratic gangs in such foundations to disenfranchise technologists more interested in innovative activities. With proper analytic tools the system knows who produces the good work and solves the problems. It determines project member rankings that drive the governance model, where the doers get the most attention when it comes to project decisions. This forms the basis of a truly meritocratic governance model where the doers get to lead.

 

Infrastructure On Demand

We eat our own dog food. This makes us users of the product we build, so we're aware of what's needed first hand. Our flagship product, Subutai Social, provides P2P Cloud and Fog capabilities through the Subutai Social Hub enabling SSF members and teams to fire up unlimited test infrastructure instantly on demand.

Collaboration Tools

Our intelligent community knowledge management system, Jarvis, allows developers to capture, organize, analyze, and share knowledge online collaboratively while researching and developing their projects. It has all of these capabilities that make innovating a pleasure at the SSF.

 

Social Communities

Open projects, whether software or hardware related, regardless of the domain, require active and healthy communities to maintain them. The SSF is community centric mainly because of this reason. Our aim is to provide the best tools for communities to easily form and collaborate naturally with each other without heavy processes and procedures. The tools handle the boring work while we use our time to tinker and innovate.

 

Cool New Technologies

Many projects at the SSF center around exciting new technologies: P2P Cloud and Fog (IoT) Computing, Big Data, Software Defined Everything, and Cyber Security. Our flagship open source project from which we derive this foundation's name is Subutai Social. It is a P2P Cloud and Fog Computing platform used to actually run the foundation itself on Linux Containers distributed globally throughout the internet.

Project Tools

We have integrated the best breed of open source tools with the best commercial tools thanks to contributions from Atlassian, and JetBrains. But we didn't stopped there. With plugins and additional custom systems like Jarvis for knowledge management, and Keshig for building automation, contiguous integration, and automated acceptance testing we've created an environment where the mundane routine work is done by machines while our members use their precious time for the joy of collaborative innovation.

Intelligent Communities

Like the movie, IronMan, Jarvis is an intelligent agent designed to make it easier for SSF communities to innovate collaboratively. Jarvis uses ontologies, and machine learning algorithms at web scale to analyze the interactions and actions of project team members with each other and with other teams. Its aim is to learn, understand, and adapt to serve our needs as we learn, research and innovate at the SSF.

Awesome Collaboration Tools

We started right using excellent tools containing incredible collaboration and integration features. Jarvis plugs into these tools to pull together the big picture while interacting with teams. Using Jarvis' browser plugin, team members swarm around a research related issue. Jarvis' browser plugin helps team members interact while finding answers online, highlighting, annotating, and tagging pages with Jarvis markers as cues. Jarvis integrates this communal research session into the knowledge base of the project.
